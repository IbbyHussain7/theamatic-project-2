Instructions to Play the Game

Setup

Players: 3 to 6 players.

The Board: The game board represents a mansion with various rooms, hallways, and secret passages.

Character Tokens: Each player selects a character and places the corresponding token on the "Start" square.

Weapon Cards: There are six weapon cards (Candlestick, Dagger, Lead Pipe, Revolver, Rope, and Wrench).

Room Cards: There are nine room cards (Kitchen, Ballroom, Conservatory, Dining Room, Lounge, Hall, Study, Library, and Billiard Room).

Murder Mystery Envelope: The game involves secret information, so three cards—one for the character, one for the weapon, and one for the room—are randomly placed into a confidential envelope at the start. This envelope holds the solution to the mystery.

Gameplay

1. Rolling the Dice

Players take turns rolling two dice to move around the board. The roll determines how many spaces you move, either horizontally or vertically, along the board’s pathways.

2. Moving Around the Board

Players can move through rooms by rolling the dice. To enter a room, you don’t need to roll exactly the number of spaces required; you can stop at the room door if your roll would take you past the room.

You can also move through corridors, but cannot move diagonally or through walls. Secret passages (between the study and the lounge, and between the conservatory and the kitchen) allow players to move between rooms more quickly.

3. Making Suggestions (Accusations)

When you land in a room, you can make a suggestion to try and figure out which character committed the murder, what weapon was used, and which room the crime took place in.

A suggestion involves the following: Choose one of the weapons from the game and suggest that the murder happened in the room you're currently in (e.g., "I suggest it was Professor Plum in the Study with the Dagger").

Other players then take turns to disprove your suggestion. If another player has one of the cards you named (i.e., the suspect, weapon, or room), they must secretly show it to you. If they don’t have any of the cards, the next player tries to disprove the suggestion.

4. Making an Accusation

At any time during the game, you can make an accusation. This is your final guess about the murderer, the weapon, and the room.

If you make a correct accusation, you win the game! If your accusation is incorrect, you are eliminated from the game but must still show cards to disprove suggestions from other players. Players continue to play until someone makes the correct accusation.

Winning the Game

The game ends when a player makes a correct accusation and reveals the contents of the envelope. The player who correctly solves the murder is the winner!

General Game Rules

Turning Over Cards: Players should keep their cards secret and not show them to others unless they are disproving a suggestion.

Movement Limitations: Players can only move into rooms by exact dice rolls, or by choosing to stay in their position and make a suggestion.

Strategy: Keep track of the cards you’ve seen and heard about throughout the game to narrow down your deductions. Observing other players' suggestions and the cards they show you can give you clues about the murder mystery.
